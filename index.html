About

I'm someone who likes understanding what actually happens inside a GPU. My approach is always bottom-up — understand the hardware before trusting any abstraction above it.

My flagship project, fast-gpu-kernels, is a public benchmark of hand-written BF16 GEMM across three NVIDIA GPU generations — A100, H100 and B200. It started on A100 (cuBLAS 83% CUDA, 80% CuTe) and is now on B200 reaching 125% of cuBLAS peak with CuTe DSL v5 (597 µs, 1478 TFLOPS peak). Uses Modal for remote GPU benchmarking.

Lately into : sm_100a (Blackwell), WGMMA, TMA barriers and pushing through the CuTe DSL optimization ladder on B200.
